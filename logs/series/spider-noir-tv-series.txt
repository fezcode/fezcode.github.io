## About

**Spider-Noir** is a live-action neo-noir superhero series developed by Oren Uziel and Steve Lightfoot for Amazon MGM Studios and Sony Pictures Television. It marks Nicolas Cage's first live-action superhero role, carrying forward — in spirit — the Spider-Man Noir he voiced in *Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse*. Set in a shadow-drenched 1930s New York, the show follows a weathered, fading private investigator who happens to be the city's only costumed hero, dragged back under the mask by a case that forces him to reckon with his own buried history.

True to its title, the series leans hard into the noir tradition: rain-slicked streets, venetian-blind shadows, a jazz-and-cigarette-smoke mood, and long stretches shot in stark black and white. Cage anchors it with a tired, hard-boiled gravity that suits the period, while producers Phil Lord, Christopher Miller, and Amy Pascal lend the project the same affection for this corner of the Spider-Man mythos that made the *Spider-Verse* films sing.

It lands at a confident **4/5**: a stylish, atmospheric, and genuinely distinctive take on a comic-book property that too rarely gets the prestige treatment. A slightly uneven pace and the occasional collision between pulpy mystery and superhero spectacle keep it from the very top of the shelf — but as a mood piece and a vehicle for Cage, it's a real treat.
