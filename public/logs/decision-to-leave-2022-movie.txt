## About

**Decision to Leave (2022)** (Korean: 헤어질 결심; RR: *Heojil gyeolsim*) is a South Korean neo-noir romantic mystery film directed by Park Chan-wook. The film follows Detective Hae-joon, a meticulous and insomniac detective, who is called to investigate the suspicious death of a man who fell from a mountain peak. He soon develops an interest in and suspicion of the dead man's mysterious wife, Seo-rae, who shows little emotion over her husband's demise. As Hae-joon delves deeper into the case, he finds himself drawn into a complex web of deceit and desire, falling in love with the enigmatic suspect.

The film is a masterful blend of detective procedural, romantic drama, and psychological thriller, showcasing Park Chan-wook's signature visual flair and intricate storytelling. It received widespread critical acclaim, with Park Chan-wook winning Best Director at the 2022 Cannes Film Festival, and was selected as the South Korean entry for the Best International Feature Film at the 95th Academy Awards.

## My Thoughts

Park Chan-wook's *Decision to Leave* is a mesmerizing, elegant, and deeply enigmatic film that solidifies his status as a cinematic master. It's a detective story wrapped in a tragic romance, unraveling with a seductive pace and a visual language that is simply stunning. Every frame is meticulously crafted, each camera movement purposeful, drawing you into a world of subtle glances, unspoken desires, and profound melancholy.

The central performances are exquisite. Park Hae-il as the obsessive detective and Tang Wei as the elusive Seo-rae deliver nuanced, layered portrayals that are magnetic. Their chemistry is a slow burn, built on intellectual sparring and emotional longing rather than overt passion, making their connection all the more compelling. The film constantly plays with your perceptions, blurring the lines between truth and deception, love and obsession.

What I adore about *Decision to Leave* is its intellectual richness and emotional complexity. It's a film that respects its audience, inviting them to piece together clues and interpret its ambiguities. The dialogue is sharp, the editing is ingenious, and the overall atmosphere is one of intoxicating mystery. It's a sophisticated, haunting, and utterly beautiful film that stays with you long after the credits roll, begging for rewatches to uncover its hidden depths. A true cinematic gem.
