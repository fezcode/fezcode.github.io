# Nier Automata & Nier Replicant

- **Category:** Game
- **Platform:** PC (Steam), PlayStation
- **Date:** 2022-04-28
- **Rating:** 5/5
- **Link:** [Nier Official Website](https://www.niergame.com/)

## About

The **Nier** series, particularly *Nier Automata* and *Nier Replicant*, are action role-playing games known for their profound philosophical narratives, unique gameplay structures, and emotionally impactful storytelling, often exploring themes of existentialism, humanity, and sacrifice.

**Nier Replicant ver.1.22474487139... (2010/2021)**: Originally released as *Nier Gestalt* (Xbox 360) and *Nier Replicant* (PlayStation 3) in Japan, with the latter featuring a younger protagonist (Nier) trying to save his sister Yonah from a mysterious disease. The 2021 "ver.1.22..." is an updated version of *Nier Replicant* that reimagines the original with modern graphics, improved combat, and additional content. The game is celebrated for its compelling story, memorable characters, haunting soundtrack, and multiple playthroughs that reveal different perspectives and true endings, making players question the nature of good and evil.

**Nier: Automata (2017)**: A standalone sequel to the original *Nier*, developed by PlatinumGames, known for their action-oriented combat. Set in a post-apocalyptic world ravaged by a proxy war between alien-created machines and human-created androids (YoRHa units), players primarily control 2B, an combat android. *Automata* expands on the philosophical themes of its predecessor, delving into questions of consciousness, free will, and the meaning of existence for both machines and androids. Its dynamic combat, stunning open world, and multiple playthrough system (each offering new character perspectives and narrative revelations) earned it widespread critical acclaim and a dedicated fanbase. The game masterfully blends various genres, from hack-and-slash to bullet-hell, and features a phenomenal orchestral soundtrack that adapts seamlessly to the gameplay.

## My Thoughts

The Nier series is an absolute masterpiece of video game storytelling. Both *Automata* and *Replicant* (especially the updated version) deliver narratives that are not just engaging, but deeply thought-provoking and emotionally devastating in the best possible way. Yoko Taro's unique directorial vision, combined with Keiichi Okabe's unforgettable musical compositions, creates an experience that transcends typical gaming.

*Nier Replicant* drew me in with its tragic hero's journey and the desperate fight to save a loved one, forcing me to confront uncomfortable truths about my actions with each subsequent playthrough. The updated combat made it a joy to revisit (or experience for the first time).

*Nier: Automata*, on the other hand, is a triumph of interactive philosophy. Its exploration of what it means to be human, set against a backdrop of beautiful ruins and existential dread, is unparalleled. The seamless shifts in gameplay perspective, the multiple endings that are essential to understanding the full story, and the sheer audacity of its narrative structure make it a truly singular achievement. The combat, as expected from PlatinumGames, is fluid and addictive, complementing the profound story perfectly.

Playing both games offers a richer understanding of the interconnected universe and reinforces the series' central message: that even in a desolate world, beauty, hope, and the human spirit (or the android approximation of it) can shine through, even if it comes at a great cost. They are games that stay with you long after the credits roll.
